# Decision Cases

A decision case is a tracked, owned record of a decision in flight. Anything that matters (mitigating a risk, approving a price change, choosing between options) runs as a case.

## What a case contains

| Field | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| Title | Short description of the decision. |
| Type | One of: risk mitigation, pricing, staffing, scope change, or general. |
| Engagement | The engagement the decision relates to. |
| Owner | The person who drives the case to a close. |
| Status | Open, In review, Approved, Declined, Applied, or Closed. |
| Linked Response | The [Response](/platform/decisions/scenarios) attached to the case. |
| Linked risk | The risk register entry it came from, if any. |
| Linked signal | The [signal](/platform/pulse/signals) that triggered it, if any. |
| Decision log | An add-only record of who said what and when. |
| Outcome | Set after a decision plays out. Did it work? |

## How cases are created

| Source | Example |
|--------|---------|
| Manual | "Should we lower rates on this engagement to save the renewal?" |
| From a signal | A critical margin pressure signal opens a case automatically. |
| From a risk | A risk register entry becomes a case once an owner is assigned. |
| From the assistant | DigitalCore suggests opening a case based on a cross-area pattern. |

## Lifecycle

1. **Open.** The case is created, the owner is set, and context is attached.
2. **In review.** A Response is generated. Options are compared.
3. **Approved or Declined.** The decision-maker chooses an Option.
4. **Applied.** The chosen Option is applied. Data updates. Follow-ups are created.
5. **Closed.** The outcome is reviewed. Lessons are captured.

# Engagements

An engagement is one delivery instance of a [service](/platform/configure/service-setup/services) for one customer or one internal team. It is where day-to-day work happens: hours go in, KPIs are recorded, margin is watched, risks are flagged.

## The engagement detail page

Clicking into an engagement opens a tabbed view.

| Tab | What you see |
|-----|--------------|
| Overview | Health score, owner, customer, contract summary. |
| Finance | Plan vs actual P&L by month, broken down by [delivery party](/concepts/glossary). |
| Performance | KPIs, SLA achievement, breach history. |
| Capacity | Hours by role, plan vs actual, leakage. |
| AI Usage | Token spend, model split, use cases. |
| Risks & Cases | Open and closed risks with linked [decision cases](/platform/decisions/cases). |
| Activity | Log of recordings, signals, and decisions. |

This is where check-ins happen. Operators usually live on Performance and Capacity. Service owners watch Finance and Risks. Customers see a curated subset through [client portals](/platform/communicate/client-portals).

## How engagements connect to everything else

Almost everything in DigitalCore attaches to an engagement: stakeholders, contracts, delivery party, plans, and recorded values across Finance, Performance, Capacity, and AI Usage. That is what makes per-engagement P&L automatic.

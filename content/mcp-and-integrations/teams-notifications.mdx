# Microsoft Teams Notifications

DigitalCore provides a Teams app that posts reports, alerts, and decision updates to channels you pick. Install it once into your tenant, then connect channels.

## Before you start

You need:

- The ability to install third-party apps in your Microsoft Teams tenant.
- A team and channel for reports and a channel for alerts.

## Install the DigitalCore Teams app

1. In DigitalCore, go to **Configure**, then **Integrations**, then **Microsoft Teams**.
2. Click **Download Teams app package**. You get a `.zip` file.
3. Open Microsoft Teams.
4. Click **Apps** in the left rail, then **Manage your apps**, then **Upload an app**.
5. Pick **Upload a custom app** and select the `.zip` file.
6. The DigitalCore app is now available in your tenant.

You only do this once per tenant.

## Connect to a channel

1. In Teams, open the channel where you want notifications.
2. @mention the **DigitalCore** bot, or add it from **Apps** in the channel.
3. The bot posts a one-time registration code, for example `DC-A3B7`.
4. Back in DigitalCore, paste the code under **Configure**, then **Integrations**, then **Microsoft Teams**.
5. The channel is now connected.

Repeat for any other channels you want to use.

## Pick channels for each notification type

For each type, pick a channel:

- **Reports.** A leadership or ops channel.
- **Alerts.** A dedicated alerts channel.
- **Decision updates.** Optional.

## What it looks like

- **Reports.** Adaptive card with headline numbers and a link.
- **Alerts.** Coloured card with the engagement, the issue, and a link.
- **Decisions.** Card with title, status, and owner.

## Mute or remove

- Mute notifications in a channel using channel notification settings.
- To stop notifications, unlink the channel under **Configure**, then **Integrations**.
- To remove the app entirely, uninstall it from Teams the same way you installed it.

# Email Notifications

## Overview

### What Email Delivery Covers

DigitalCore can send the following via email:

- **Scheduled report delivery** — When a scheduled report generates, a notification (with optional PDF attachment) is sent to configured recipients
- **SLA breach alerts** — Direct email notification when a performance threshold is crossed
- **AI insight notifications** — Periodic summaries from the AI agent

Email delivery is separate from in-app notification preferences (which you configure in **Settings → Notifications**). Email channels here are used for structured, automated report delivery.

---

## Channel Types: Email vs. Email List

DigitalCore provides two email channel types:

| Type | Best For |
|------|----------|
| **Email** | Single recipient — a named stakeholder or distribution address |
| **Email List** | Managed group — link a set of internal or external stakeholders who all receive the same reports |

---

## Adding an Email Channel

### Single Email

1. Go to **Communicate → Channels**
2. Click **Add Channel** and select **Email**
3. Enter the recipient email address and a display name for the channel (e.g. "Monthly Report — Client ABC")
4. Click **Add**

Single email channels are immediately active — no OAuth or verification step is required.

### Email List

1. Go to **Communicate → Channels**
2. Click **Add Channel** and select **Email List**
3. Select the stakeholders to include from your organisation's stakeholder list

Stakeholders listed here are pulled from your engagement contacts. To add someone who is not yet a stakeholder, first add them as a stakeholder in the relevant engagement, then return to add them to the email list.

---

## Assigning to a Scheduled Report

1. Go to **Communicate → Reports** and open any schedule
2. Under **Delivery Channels**, check the email channel(s) you want to include
3. Under **Email Recipients** (if shown), you can also add one-off addresses directly on the schedule — these are separate from saved channels
4. Save the schedule

When the schedule runs, the report notification is sent to all selected channels and any directly entered addresses.

---

## PDF Attachments

Scheduled reports support optional PDF export. When PDF generation is enabled on a schedule, the generated PDF is attached to the email delivery automatically.

To enable:
1. Open the schedule in **Communicate → Reports**
2. Turn on the **Generate PDF** toggle
3. Save

> PDF generation adds a few seconds to the processing time. For time-sensitive delivery windows, account for this delay.

---

## Managing Email Channels

### Updating Recipients

Edit the channel from **Communicate → Channels** to change the email address (for single email channels) or update the stakeholder list (for email list channels).

### Removing a Channel

Delete the channel from **Communicate → Channels**. This removes the channel from all schedules it was assigned to. Recipients will no longer receive scheduled deliveries from DigitalCore.

---

## Notification Preferences vs. Delivery Channels

There are two separate email configuration areas in DigitalCore:

| Setting | Location | Purpose |
|---------|----------|---------|
| **Notification preferences** | Settings → Notifications | Controls whether *you* receive email alerts for events like SLA breaches or check-in reminders |
| **Delivery channels** | Communicate → Channels | Controls where *scheduled reports* are delivered (email, Slack, Teams) |

If you are not receiving scheduled report emails, check both areas — the delivery channel must be configured on the schedule, and the generating user's notification preferences do not affect channel delivery.

## Troubleshooting

### Emails are not arriving

Check these in order:

1. **Spam / junk folder** — Automated report emails may initially land in spam. Mark as "Not spam" and add `notifications@digitalcore.app` to your contacts
2. **Delivery channel is assigned** — Go to **Communicate → Reports**, open the schedule, and confirm the email channel is checked under **Delivery Channels**
3. **Schedule is active** — Confirm the schedule shows a **Next Run** date
4. **Email address is correct** — Edit the channel and verify the address

### Email arrives but PDF is missing

PDF generation may have failed for the report. Check:

1. Open the report in **Communicate → Reports**
2. If a PDF failed to generate, there will be an error indicator on the report entry
3. Use the **Regenerate** option to trigger a new run

PDF generation can fail if the report contains data that cannot be rendered (e.g. corrupt chart data). Regenerating the report sections usually resolves this.

### "Recipient address rejected"

This means the receiving mail server rejected the delivery. Possible causes:

- The email address has a typo — edit the channel and correct the address
- The recipient's mail server is blocking automated email — ask the recipient's IT team to allowlist `digitalcore.app`
- The address is a restricted internal distribution list that does not accept external senders

### Stakeholders not appearing in Email List picker

Stakeholders are sourced from engagement contacts. If a person is not showing:

- Check they are added as a stakeholder on at least one engagement in your organisation
- Contact stakeholders must have a valid email address in their profile

### Changing the sender address

Report delivery emails are sent from DigitalCore's centralised sending domain (`notifications@digitalcore.app`). Custom sender domains are not currently supported on standard plans.

# Notification Channels

## Overview

### What Are Notification Channels?

Notification channels are the delivery targets DigitalCore uses to push reports, alerts, and AI-generated summaries to your team. Instead of logging in to check for new information, the intelligence comes to you — in the tools your team already uses.

Once connected, channels can be assigned to:

- **Scheduled reports** — Automatically deliver generated reports to one or more channels at the time they run
- **SLA breach alerts** — Notify channels when a performance threshold is crossed
- **AI insight notifications** — Push proactive intelligence from the AI agent

---

## Accessing Notification Channels

Navigate to **Communicate → Channels** in the main navigation. This page shows all configured channels for your organisation, their connection status, and lets you add, test, or remove channels.

---

## Channel Types

| Type | Description | Setup Method |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Slack** | Deliver to any Slack channel your bot has access to | OAuth — one-click connect |
| **Microsoft Teams** | Deliver to Teams channels and group chats | App package install + registration code |
| **Email** | Send delivery to a single address | Add email address directly |
| **Email List** | Broadcast to a managed list of stakeholders | Add stakeholder group |

---

## How Channels Connect to Scheduled Reports

When you create or edit a scheduled report (**Communicate → Reports**), the schedule editor shows a list of your configured notification channels. Select one or more channels to include — when the report runs and is generated, DigitalCore will deliver it to each selected channel automatically.

Channels must be connected and verified before they can be selected on a schedule. The channel list in the schedule editor only shows verified channels.

---

## Channel Health

Each channel shows a status indicator:

- **Verified** — The channel has been tested and is ready to receive deliveries
- **Connected** — The integration is authorised but has not yet been verified with a test message
- **Failed** — A recent delivery attempt failed. Check the troubleshooting guide for your channel type

Channels with repeated failures are automatically paused to avoid noisy error logs. Re-verify to re-activate.
